**Day 2**
The four types of editing nobody explains:
→ Developmental: structure + argument
→ Line: voice + clarity + rhythm
→ Copy: grammar + consistency
→ Proof: final typo sweep
You probably don't need all four at full price. Know what you actually need. #WritingCommunity #AmWriting

**Day 3**
If an editor returns a "comprehensive developmental edit" in 72 hours for a 60,000-word manuscript, that's AI output billed at human rates. A real developmental edit takes 20–40 hours minimum. The math always tells you something. #Editing #AuthorsOnTwitter

**Day 4**
Your first chapter is doing too much work. It's trying to establish voice, introduce the premise, hook the reader, and signal the structure — all at once. Give it one job. The rest follows. #AmWriting #WritingCommunity

**Day 5**
Real editorial feedback is located. It references a specific page, a specific paragraph, a specific line. If the feedback you received could apply to any book in your genre without changing a word — it wasn't written for yours. #Editing #AuthorsOnTwitter

**Day 6**
Most first-time nonfiction authors have the same three problems:
1. One chapter in the wrong place
2. An argument that starts but doesn't land
3. Prose that's correct but not alive
All three are fixable. None of them are reasons the book can't be published. #SelfPublishing #WritingCommunity

**Day 8**
Reading your manuscript out loud is the fastest way to find rhythm problems your eye skips over. If you stumble reading your own sentence, your reader will stumble too. Record yourself. Listen back. Trust what you hear. #AmWriting #WritingCommunity

**Day 9**
The chapter you love most is probably your biggest structural problem. Every author has one. The chapter that feels essential because you worked hardest on it — is often the one slowing everything down. Be honest about it. #AmWriting #Editing

**Day 10**
Five AI slop phrases to cut from your manuscript immediately:
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It's more important than ever..."
- "Delve into..."
- "In conclusion..."
- [Sentence that starts with "Additionally"]
These signals say "AI wrote this." Rewrite them. #WritingCommunity #AmWriting

**Day 11**
The difference between a good book and a great book is usually one thing: voice. Not research. Not structure. Not even story. Voice is what makes a reader feel like they're in the room with you. It's also the hardest thing to self-edit. #AmWriting #AuthorsOnTwitter

**Day 12**
You've probably spent more on writing courses than on editing your actual book. The course taught you to write. Editing is what makes your writing publishable. The sequence matters. Invest in the work, not just the skills. #SelfPublishing #Editing

**Day 13**

If they say no, keep looking.

If the sample feedback is vague, keep looking.

Specific + located = real feedback. #AuthorsOnTwitter #Editing

**Day 14**

The honest hierarchy for editorial spending:
1. Structure (most important)
2. Voice (high return)
3. Grammar (AI handles most of this well)

**Day 17**
Most manuscripts have a "false ending" — a conclusion that feels like the landing but isn't. The real landing is 2–3 pages later, buried under what feels like summary. Cut to it. Your reader will thank you. #AmWriting #WritingCommunity

**Day 18**
Something most editors won't tell you: you can self-edit grammar. ProWritingAid. Grammarly. Even Word's built-in AI. These tools catch 80% of mechanical errors. Where you actually need a human (or premium AI): structure and voice. #Editing #AuthorsOnTwitter

**Day 19**
A sentence is too long when the reader has to re-read it to understand it. That's the test. Not word count. Not number of clauses. Did your reader have to start over? Cut until they don't. #AmWriting #WritingCommunity

**Day 20**
Three questions to ask yourself before hiring any editor:
1. Can I see sample output before I pay?
2. What's their timeline — and can the math support it?
3. Do they specialize in my type of book?
If any of these don't land cleanly, keep looking. #AuthorsOnTwitter #Editing

**Day 22**
Reverse-outline your manuscript. Read chapter by chapter. Write one sentence describing what each chapter actually does (not what you intended). If a chapter doesn't have a clear sentence, it's either doing too much or not enough. #AmWriting #WritingCommunity

**Day 23**
The sensitivity read isn't about making your book inoffensive. It's about making sure you haven't accidentally alienated the readers you're writing for. The best authors invite this feedback. The best editors deliver it with specificity and respect. #WritingCommunity #Editing

**Day 24**
