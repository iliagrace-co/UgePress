# Uge Press — Logo Concepts

*Three concepts. All restrained. All scalable. All designed to age well.*

---

## Concept 1: The Wave Mark (Recommended)

**Nickname:** The Crest

**Concept:**
A single, clean wave-form glyph sits to the left of the wordmark. Not a decorative ocean wave — something closer to a typographic flourish, a single fluid curve that completes itself. Imagine the underline of a signature, or the tail of an elegant italic letter, made geometric. It references water without being literal.

**Construction:**
- The mark is a single Bézier curve: begins thin, swells slightly at the crest, tapers to a fine point at the right — like a macron (ˉ) with life in it
- Width: exactly the same as the "U" in "Uge" — the mark and the word feel unified
- Positioned left of wordmark, vertically centered to the cap height of "U"

**Wordmark:**
- "UGE PRESS" — all caps, Cormorant Garamond, tracked at +120 (wide spacing, aristocratic)
- Weight: Regular (400), not bold — confidence doesn't need to shout
- "UGE" and "PRESS" on the same baseline, separated by a thin em-space
- Optional: "PRESS" in slightly smaller size (80% of "UGE"), same weight — creates hierarchy without breaking unity

**Color:**
- Primary: Charcoal (#1a1a1a) on Cream (#FAF8F5)
- Reversed: Cream (#FAF8F5) on Charcoal (#1a1a1a) for dark contexts
- Accent version: Wave mark in Terracotta (#C45D3E), wordmark in Charcoal — for editorial use, print collateral

**Favicon/Icon:**
- The wave mark alone, Terracotta on Cream, inside a minimal square frame with generous padding
- At 16px: renders as a single elegant stroke — distinctive, not muddy

**Why it works:**
The wave is Brooke's symbol. Made geometric, it becomes architectural — like a publisher's colophon, not a beach logo. The wide-tracked caps give it the quiet authority of Penguin or Gallimard. It doesn't try to explain itself.

## Concept 2: The Monogram U (Secondary Option)

**Nickname:** The Sigil

**Concept:**
A single stylized "U" serves as the brand mark. But this isn't a font letter — it's a drawn glyph where the two vertical strokes taper slightly outward at the base (like a chalice, or the opening of a book), and the connecting curve at the bottom carries one subtle notch — an implied wave crest, visible only on close inspection.

**Construction:**
- Custom-drawn letterform, not set in any font
- Proportions inspired by classical serif capital U: tall, narrow, confident
- The inner curve at the base of the U rises slightly in the middle — a ghost of a wave
- Stroke weight: medium, consistent (no stress contrast) — geometric, not calligraphic

**Wordmark pairing:**
- "UGE PRESS" in Cormorant Garamond, tracked at +80
- Mark sits above or to the left of wordmark
- For header use: mark left, wordmark right, vertically centered

**Color:**
- Same palette as Concept 1
- The monogram is particularly effective in Terracotta — bold enough to own a page, restrained enough to belong on one

**Favicon/Icon:**
- The U-sigil alone, centered, minimal padding
- Works at any size from 16px to billboard

**Why it works:**
Monograms have been the mark of printers and publishers for 500 years. The hidden wave gives initiates a small pleasure of discovery. It's the kind of logo that gets more interesting the longer you look at it.

## Concept 3: The Rule Mark (Editorial Option)

**Nickname:** The Press Mark

**Concept:**
No symbol. Pure typography, elevated by a single design decision: a thin horizontal rule (hairline, 0.5px) runs the full width of the wordmark, positioned between "UGE" and "PRESS" — like the rule that separates a headline from a dateline in a broadsheet, or the space between title and author on a book spine.

**Construction:**
- "UGE" in Cormorant Garamond, 600 weight (semi-bold), tracked at +60
- Horizontal rule: hairline, same width as the text block, 4px below "UGE" baseline
- "PRESS" in Inter, weight 300, uppercase, tracked at +200 (very wide) — a counterpoint material to "UGE"
- The tension between the serif and the sans-serif, separated by the rule, IS the logo

**Color:**
- Rule in Terracotta (#C45D3E) — the one moment of color, doing all the work
- Type in Charcoal

**Favicon/Icon:**
- The rule alone (a thin Terracotta stroke) on Cream — minimal, bold as punctuation
- Or: "UP" monogram in Cormorant Garamond, closely tracked

**Why it works:**
This concept says "we are a publishing house" more directly than any graphic symbol. The editorial rule is the oldest mark in print — it organizes, it separates, it commands. The Terracotta line is the accent color doing its highest-value work: not decorating, but structuring.

---

## Recommendation

**Lead with Concept 1.** The wave mark is ownable, scalable, and carries Brooke's personal symbol without being sentimental about it. It has the functional elegance of a well-designed colophon.

**Keep Concept 3 as a text-only variant** for contexts where the mark doesn't reproduce well (small print, embossing, legal documents).

**Concept 2 is the right direction for a future rebrand** if Uge Press grows into a full publishing imprint with multiple editors — the monogram would scale across a family of marks.
